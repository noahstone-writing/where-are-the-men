Why Single Women Vastly Outnumber Men on the Mission Field

Honoring the Women

Who is already on the field

  • About two thirds of missionaries are married couples
  • Of the singles, 70 to 80 percent are single women
  • Crossworld: single women outnumber single men four to one
  • In high-risk areas, reported as high as seven to one
  • In Central Asia, over my own decade, five or more women to every man

The Cost They Carry

Seven challenges single women face

  • Longing for a godly husband who shares the calling, with few men on the field
  • Loneliness and isolation where deep friendship is hard to sustain
  • Suspicion, restricted movement, and a constant need for extra protection
  • Heavier spiritual and emotional loads when couples leave the field early

The Cost They Carry

Seven challenges, continued

  • Last in the team's order of influence, often feeling like an afterthought
  • Grieving marriage prospects the ratio itself has taken
  • Higher burnout risk and harder re-entry without family support

The Gift of Singleness

Not a waiting room, but a strategic gift

  • "To the unmarried and the widows I say that it is good for them to remain single as I am." (1 Corinthians 7:8)
  • Freedom to go to hard places, move quickly, and take real risks
  • Undivided devotion to the Lord in frontier and creative access work

Interlude

A Testimony

Now we hear from a sister in Christ who has lived these realities

Where Are the Men?

Now the honest question

  • IMB President Chitwood, official: single women outnumber single men two to one
  • Best survey, Missio Nexus: 74 percent of single long term missionaries are women, about 2.85 to one
  • Across faith missions: 70 to 80 percent of singles are women, roughly four to one
  • OMF: single men are 25 percent of the unreached, but 1 percent of the force

Seven Barriers

Why so few young men, part one

  • The pastoral track pulls our most zealous men toward the pulpit, not the field
  • Provider anxiety makes living on raised support feel impossible
  • Student debt collides with agency no debt policies
  • Marriage first, and marriage often becomes the exit rather than the gateway

Seven Barriers

Why so few young men, part two

  • Shame and disqualification from widespread pornography use
  • Parents, even believing parents, quietly steer sons toward safety
  • Missions framed as a short trip, not a lifelong calling

The Clock Is Not Neutral

Who will send the next generation?

  • "In 20-30 years … who on earth will be supporting missions?" — Chandelle Body, 2015
  • Missions Fest Seattle: about 75% of the room over fifty, more than half over 65
  • Not only who will go — who will pray, give, and send
  • Raise them up now, or the base and the force gray out together

First and Foremost

The agencies point back to the local church

  • "I'm convinced the lack of young men on the mission field is first and foremost a local church issue." (Paul Akin)
  • Older men discipling young men with the nations in mind
  • Showing the next generation that God is a missionary God

Where Our Church Must Own It

Eight areas, part one

  • Partnership: build strategic ties with established sending agencies
  • Pipeline: a clear path to identify, disciple, test, train, and send
  • Polity: agreed policies for financial, prayer, and member care
  • Non traditional workers: real backing for creative access roles

Where Our Church Must Own It

Eight areas, part two

  • Exposure trips that take our people to ministry in hard places
  • The strategic gap: toward North Africa, the Middle East, and Central Asia
  • Preaching and parents that commission young men and young couples
  • Professional pathways: doctors, engineers, and business as mission

The Whole Church Responding

Three charges, one body

  • To the women: serve with excellence and spur the men toward bold obedience
  • To the men: count the cost, repent of comfort, and go or send at great cost
  • To the whole church: build pipelines, form partnerships, send to the unreached
  • The harvest is still plentiful. God is still calling. May we be found faithful.
